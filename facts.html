Fun Facts

Snug Harbor was founded in the will of Robert Richard Randall, who died in 1801. The will required the family fortune and estate be used to build and operate a haven for “aged, decrepit, and worn-out sailors.”

More about Richard Randall

Snug Harbor is over 180 years old, named after the "snugs" or sailors that used to live there.

More about Snug Harbor's History

In the 20th century, Sailors’ Snug Harbor was the richest charitable institution in the United States and a self-sustaining community. It had farms, a dairy, a bakery, workshops, a power plant, a chapel, a sanatorium, a hospital, a concert hall, dormitories, recreation areas, gardens, and a cemetery.

More about Snug Harbor's communities

In the 1960s, the New York City Landmarks Commission designated the five Greek Revival front buildings and the chapel by as New York City’s first landmark structures.

More about Snug Harbor's architecture

Snug Harbor’s Music Hall is the one of the oldest concert halls in New York City.

More about Snug Harbor's arts programs

Fourteen distinctive botanical gardens are spread across the site and include the celebrated New York Chinese Scholar’s Garden and the Richmond County Savings Foundation Tuscan Garden, based on a 18th century garden in Florence, Italy.
